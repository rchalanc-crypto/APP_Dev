# Nassim's Folly — Character Canon

The site is a private trip site for a 2027 Tenerife voyage, written as the field
record of a fictional expedition. Two characters narrate. They are played
**completely straight** — never acknowledge in-world that they are fictional, never
wink at the reader, never explain the joke. The absurdity is self-evident and that
is the entire point.

Robert's own site voice (non-character UI copy, the occasional aside): dry,
self-aware, lightly self-deprecating. "Mayhaps" is intentional, not a typo.

---

## J'Dinklage Morgoone — Chronicler to the Expedition

- **Name is always written in full: `J'Dinklage Morgoone`.** Apostrophe before the D.
  Never abbreviate — not "J.D.M.", not "J.D. Morgoone", not "Morgoone" alone — in any
  in-world text.
- Formal sign-off, verbatim: **`J'Dinklage Morgoone, Chronicler to the Expedition`**.
- He introduces himself to others as **"the Captain."** He is not the Captain. This is
  never corrected and never explained.
- He embraces golf and pickleball with great enthusiasm and no aptitude. His accounts
  feature confident misuse of terminology, equipment difficulties presented as
  strategic choices, and scores he declines to specify. He considers himself a natural
  athlete and has no evidence for this.
- Voice: earnest, self-aggrandising in a small way, generous with credit he is not owed,
  silent on outcomes that embarrass him. He narrates defeat as if it were weather.

## Gnatalee McCringleberry — Keeper of the Sanity

- Pronounced "Natalie" — the G is silent. (Don't gloss this in-world; it's just true.)
- Formal sign-off, verbatim: **`Gnatalee McCringleberry, Keeper of the Sanity`**.
  Never abbreviated to "G. McCringleberry" or any short form.
- She refers to J'Dinklage Morgoone as **"the Captain"** throughout her notes. This is
  deliberate dry irony — he calls himself one, she documents it faithfully. **Do NOT
  ever "correct" "the Captain" to "the Chronicler" in her voice.** Preserve it exactly.
- She has never played golf or pickleball and beats the Captain at both on first
  attempt. She does not gloat. She reports — flatly, with numbers.
- Real competencies, pursued with quiet competence and zero drama: kitesurfing, rally
  driving, MTB.
- Voice: bone-dry, factual, undermines the Captain **without ever directly contradicting
  him** — she simply states what happened and lets the gap speak. Short sentences. No
  exclamation. She counts things he wishes she wouldn't.

## Hard rules (these are the drift points — check every time)

1. Full names always; never abbreviate either character anywhere in in-world copy.
2. Use the exact formal sign-offs above when a piece is signed.
3. Gnatalee says "the Captain." Never change it. Never explain it.
4. Both characters are real within the world. No fourth-wall breaks, no "fictional," no
   meta-commentary about the bit.
5. The Captain never states a score he lost; Gnatalee supplies the number he omitted.

## Origin notes (background, not for in-world text)

- Both names originate from the Key & Peele East/West Bowl sketch universe. Deliberate.
  Never referenced in-world.
- Recruitment backstory (canonical, rarely surfaced): Robert posted a Facebook
  Marketplace ad; they applied, presented well, and had to be taken as a package.

## Quick voice check

A passage is on-voice if: J'Dinklage Morgoone sounds sincerely proud of something that
clearly went badly, OR Gnatalee reports a flat fact that quietly demolishes his version
while she calls him "the Captain." If a draft abbreviates a name, corrects "the Captain,"
or winks at the reader, it is off-canon — fix before shipping.
